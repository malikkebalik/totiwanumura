The Summer I Turned Pretty Actor Christopher Briney Bio | Wiki

Christopher Briney (age: 25, March 24, 1998) is a stunning American actor who has been gaining immense attention since he made his romantic presence alongside Lola Tung in the Amazon series “The Summer I Turned Pretty.”

In “The Summer I Turned Pretty,” casting directors David Rapaport, Lyndsey Baldasare, and Kimberly Wistedt put him in the character of Conrad, who falls in love with his best friend “Belly,” but surprisingly, his on-screen brother Jeremiah (Gavin Casalegno) also began liking Belly.

On the big screen, You have seen Christopher may be the first time in “The Summer I Turned Pretty,” but he is not new in the acting industry because before playing the role of “Conrad.”

He worked in “Want This,” “Steph,” “A/B Scenes,” “Racing the Clock,” “invisible,” and “Under the Covers” short films, and another surprise for his fans is that Briney will appear in the character of “James” in Director Mary Harron film “Dali Land,” which is right now in post-production.

Christopher Briney Birthday & Family

The 6 feet tall stunning actor Christopher Briney was born on March 24, 1998, to his mother and father, Michael Briney, in the Connecticut State of US, where he lived his life most of the time with his parents and friends.

Being a rising star in the entertainment industry, his parents have yet to emerge in the media, but he has uploaded their picture on his instgram.

In his hometown, Chris spent much of his childhood playing baseball and riding his bike around town. It wasn’t until he attended the Waterbury Arts Magnet School and took an acting class by chance that he decided to pursue acting as a career.

Acting Career

To pursue a career in the acting profession, in 2016, he relocated to New York and enrolled at Pace University, where he earned a BFA in Acting for Film, TV, Voiceover, and Commercial in May 2020; while studying at Pace University, he worked in “Twelfth Night,” “Hamlet,” “Radium Girls,” “As Bees in Honey Drown,” “The Library Play,” “Pig Farm,” and “If We Were Birds” theater productions.

While Briney was making his career as a theatre artist at the same time, he was trying to get the chance to take a step on the big screen; that’s why in 2018, he first appeared in “Under the Covers” and in 2020 in “Want This,” and with that, he continued his theater career and took Acting training from Brian Hastert, Johnny Wu, KK Moggie, Matthew Humphreys, Christina Pumariega, Nidra Sous la Terre, and Amelia Campbell.
