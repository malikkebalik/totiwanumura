Carlos Condit Early Life

Carlos Condit was born on April 26, 1984, in Albuquerque, New Mexico, and is of Austrian, German, Native American, and Mexican descent. Condit’s father, Brian, was the Chief of Staff for former New Mexico governor and Democratic presidential candidate Bill Richardson. Condit attended Cibola High School on Albuquerque’s West Side. He began wrestling at the age of nine and continued throughout high school, and started training in Gaidojutsu under Greg Jackson at fifteen. Condit turned professional at eighteen years old, taking less than a minute to submit Nick Roscorta with a rear-naked choke in his debut in Juárez, Mexico on September 6, 2002. He faced off with ninety-five fight veteran and Shoot Boxing World Tournament 2002 champion Andy Souwer in Tokyo, Japan on September 23, 2003.

Carlos Condit Career

Carlos Condit made his debut for the WEC on January 20, 2007, at WEC 25 where he defeated Kyle Jensen in the first round via submission. Condit faced Brock Larson on August 5, 2007, at WEC 29. Condit won the fight via first-round submission. He faced Hiromitsu Miura on August 3, 2008, at WEC 35. Carlos won the back and forth fight via TKO in the fourth round, winning Fight of the Night honors. He made his promotional debut on April 1, 2009, at UFC Fight Night 18 facing Martin Kampmann. Condit was expected to face Chris Lytle on February 27, 2011, at UFC 127. He faced Georges St-Pierre on November 17, 2012, at UFC 154. Carlos faced Martin Kampmann in a rematch on August 28, 2013, at UFC Fight Night 27. Condit next fought Tyron Woodley in the co-main event at UFC 171.

An MRI confirmed that Condit had suffered a torn ACL and partially torn meniscus which would require surgery. Condit was expected to face current champion Robbie Lawler on November 15, 2015, at UFC 193. After a sixteen-month layoff, Condit returned to the UFC and faced Neil Magny on December 30, 2017, at UFC 219. Condit was expected to face Matt Brown on April 14, 2018, at UFC on Fox 29. Condit faced Michael Chiesa on December 29, 2018, at UFC 232. He lost the fight via submission in the second round. After nearly two years away from the sport, Condit faced Court McGee on October 4, 2020, at UFC on ESPN: Holm vs. Aldana. Condit faced Matt Brown on January 16, 2021, at UFC on ABC 1. Condit faced Max Griffin on July 10, 2021, at UFC 264.

Carlos Condit Personal Life

Carlos Condit married his longtime girlfriend, Seager Marie McCullah, in December 2010. The couple welcomed their first child, a son, in March 2010. Carlos partnered with Los Angeles brand Sene to launch a custom denim collaboration in November 2020. On September 17, 2021, it was announced that Condit has retired from competing in MMA. Carlos is a Black belt in Gaidojutsu, a Brown belt in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu, Purple belt in 10th Planet Jiu-Jitsu.
