As of his career, Trey Songz begins his career in 2003, when he signs a recording contract with Atlantic Records. Prior to the release of his debut album in 2004, he released many mixtapes under the moniker “Prince of Virginia.” “I Gotta Make It,” his debut album, was released on July 26, 2005.

His breakthrough song, “Gotta Make It,” featuring Twista, was released in March 2005, and he began production on a follow-up album with frequent collaborator Troy Taylor in mid-2006. The lead single from his second studio album, “Wonder Woman,” was published in February 2007, while his second studio album, “Trey Day,” was released on October 2, 2007.

He released his third studio album, “Ready,” on August 31, 2009, and it debuted at #3 on the Billboard 200, selling 131,000 copies in its first week. Passion, Pain & Pleasure, his fourth studio album, was released on September 14, 2010. In July 2011, he was cast as Ryan in the film “Texas Chainsaw 3D,” playing the male lead role.

On January 4th, 2013, the film was released. On August 21, 2012, Atlantic Records released his fifth studio album “Chapter V,” and on July 1, 2014, he released his sixth album “Trigga,” which opened at number one on the US Billboard 200 with first-week sales of 105,000 copies.

Songz released his seventh studio album, “Tremaine The Album,” on March 24, 2017, and two surprise mixtapes, 11 and 28, on November 28, 2018, in honor of his 34th birthday. He also appeared in the 2019 film “Sex Crimes” as Professor Lombardo.

Trey also released “Back Home” starring Summer Walker on April 29, 2020, as well as “2020 Riots: How Many Times” on April 29, 2020. Songz released his second single, “Circles,” on August 14th of the following year. Back Home, his eighth album was released on October 9th, 2020.
